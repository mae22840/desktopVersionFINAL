Dracaena Draco

The Dragontree (Dracaena Draco) is a striking, slow-growing tree native to the Canary Islands, Cape Verde, and parts of Morocco. Known for its unique, spiky leaves and thick, gnarled trunk, the Dragontree can reach impressive heights, making it a dramatic focal point in any space. Its silvery-blue, sword-shaped leaves grow in rosettes at the tips of the branches, giving the plant a distinct and architectural look.

The Dragontree prefers bright, indirect light and can tolerate some direct sunlight, although it thrives in a slightly shaded spot. It is relatively low-maintenance and drought-tolerant, making it an excellent choice for those looking for a hardy, indoor tree. It is best to allow the soil to dry out between waterings, as the Dragontree is susceptible to root rot in overly wet conditions.

This plant is known for its resilience and unique appearance, making it a fantastic addition to modern and tropical-themed interiors. With its slow growth and sturdy structure, the Dragontree is perfect for adding a touch of drama and architectural beauty to any room.

In addition to its beauty, the Dragontree is also famous for its resin, known as "dragon's blood," which has been historically used in dyes, varnishes, and incense. With its unique foliage and minimal care needs, the Dragontree is a perfect choice for both plant enthusiasts and beginners alike.
